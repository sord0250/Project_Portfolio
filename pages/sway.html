Work in progress

Sway

Sway is a nature wellness app currently in progress. It is being shaped as a product centered on daily outdoor activity, stress reduction, and a more engaging wellness experience supported by an artistic visual direction.

Problem

Why Sway exists

18-34-year-olds report average stress of 6 out of 10, compared to 3.4 among adults 65 and older. Most wellness apps run on the same playbook (streaks, badges, daily goals) because it drives retention. Users describe losing a 200-day streak and feeling worse, not better.

Research

How I validated it

There is a lot of statistical evidence that highlight the healing and relaxing aspects of nature. Simply being in nature for 10 minutes can reduce stress and improve mood. Our entire app is built around these findings. Get people to a place where they connect with nature on a regular basis.

Decisions

What shaped the product

  1. 1
    Studio Ghibli-inspired visuals:The app needed to mirror the calming and peaceful aspects of nature, and no art style does that better in my opinion than Studio Ghibli.
  2. 2
    Flexible 'goal' setting:The app shouldn't cause stress. Users can't loose their streaks, it simply tracks minutes and total days outside. They also decide how many days they want to try to get outside per week. Our reminders will work around that.
  3. 3
    Nature Sounds:For days that are rainy or users are too difficult to get outside, the app provides calming nature sounds to re-create that calming environment. This still counts towards their goals.
  4. 4
    Your Personal Nature Map:When a user logs their outdoor activities, the app tracks (with permission) their location. Over time, this data fills their personal map to show them where their calm spots are.
What I learned Product Principles

The biggest thing I've learned building Sway is ruthless focus on the problem: if a feature doesn't solve it, it doesn't belong in the MVP. The clearest example was a nature journal idea I wanted to build, until my friend pointed out it didn't actually serve the real goal of getting people outside consistently each week, so I cut it. That same discipline showed up when I chose a single daily observation prompt over streak-based engagement, and again in my onboarding research, where studying Snapchat, Duolingo, and Peach taught me how much to ask of a user before they've felt any value, which shaped Sway's onboarding flow that skips account creation and leads with value instead.
