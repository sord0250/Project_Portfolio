Work in progress

Sway

Sway is a nature wellness app currently in progress. It is being shaped as a cross-platform product centered on daily outdoor activity, stress reduction, and a more engaging wellness experience supported by a retro-inspired visual direction.

Video placeholder

Problem

Why Sway exists

Wellness apps often focus too narrowly on tracking instead of helping people build a habit they actually want to use. Sway is being designed to make outdoor activity feel more motivating, more visual, and more approachable for a broader audience.

Solution

A nature-first wellness experience

The product roadmap focuses on habit formation, daily engagement, and measurable wellness outcomes. The goal is to pair useful wellness features with a calmer brand and a more distinctive look and feel.

Product Creation

What I created so far

  • Validated the concept with user and market research.
  • Defined the MVP and product roadmap.
  • Built a cross-platform MVP with React Native and Expo.
  • Aligned branding, UX, and product direction.

AI-assisted development

How AI supported the build

AI has mostly supported research, stack decisions, and early visual exploration while the product is still being built. Deep Research helped with user and market research, Claude and ChatGPT helped me think through the stack and setup, Codex implemented the development environment, and ChatGPT Images plus Canva helped with mock greenery, imagery, and logo ideation.

Market Research

ChatGPT Deep Research, Claude, and Perplexity were used for research on users, competing products, and demand using real data.

Stack selection

Claude and ChatGPT helped compare technical options and refine the setup for a cross-platform wellness app.

Implementation

Codex built the development environment, while ChatGPT, Claude, and image tools helped with setup, mock visuals, greenery, and logo ideation.

Implementation

Current build direction

The app is being developed as a cross-platform React Native and Expo product, with ongoing testing and feedback shaping the current build.

Branding

Visual direction

The branding is leaning into a retro, nature-focused look that supports the broader wellness concept and helps the app feel more inviting than a typical fitness product.
