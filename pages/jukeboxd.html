Social music platform

Jukeboxd

Jukeboxd is a social music review platform built to feel more vibey and unique than most music review apps. The goal was to appeal to a broader audience by pairing a retro-inspired brand with a social experience for discovering, reviewing, and talking about music.

Video placeholder

Problem

Why Jukeboxd exists

Most music review platforms feel narrow, overly serious, or designed for people already deep into the hobby. Jukeboxd was meant to open that experience up with a more approachable social layer and a visual style that feels more fun, retro, and inviting.

Solution

A more vibey music review app

The platform combines music reviews, social interaction, and a retro design direction so the product feels distinct from the usual music community apps. The intent was to create something broader audiences could enjoy without feeling like they needed to be power users first.

Product Creation

What I created

  • Defined the product vision and core experience for Jukeboxd.
  • Created the database structure and SQL needed to support the app.
  • Shaped the retro visual identity, color palette, and UI in Figma.
  • Used user interviews and feedback to guide the final direction.

AI-assisted development

How AI supported the build

AI played a lighter role on Jukeboxd until the frontend design stage. The visual direction and color scheme were created in Figma, then Codex helped turn that design into working CSS. AI also supported market research and helped confirm or challenge feature ideas as the concept evolved.

Product design

AI acted as a technical refiner, double-checking our frontend decisions and helping improve the design implementation.

Market Research

I used ChatGPT's deep research feature to gather reports on users and other products using real data.

Implementation

Figma defined the look and feel, and Codex translated that frontend design into actual CSS for the page.

Implementation

Technical foundation

The database structure was designed to support the frontend experience and keep the app flexible as features grew. Relational schema choices and SQL queries were part of the early planning so the product could scale beyond a simple prototype.

Audience fit

How the product direction evolved

Interviews and feedback helped validate the appeal of a broader, more playful music review experience, while the retro design language helped differentiate Jukeboxd from more conventional music platforms.
